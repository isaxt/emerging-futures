The Aesthetics Manifested Through Collages

This space hosts a series of digital collages that explore the intersection of sacred tradition and evolving technological advancements. It juxtaposes religious iconography with contemporary tech and scientific imagery. The work draws from diverse spiritual sources—Catholic relics and Chinese spiritual imagery—placing them in visual dialogue with the tools and symbols of modern innovation, like microchips, Arduino boards, anatomical diagrams, and data visualizations. The collages interrogate the evolving nature of faith, transcendence, and human augmentation in an increasingly technologized world.

Lotus Protocol, 2025

Between the circuit board and the last name seals lies a question older than silicon: Can wisdom be transmitted digitally? The lotus blooms against a matrix of code—neither corrupted by it nor separate from it. Chinese calligraphy flows like algorithms across the blue, each character a compressed file of centuries. Spiritual heritage doesn't resist technological transformation; it asks whether transformation was always the nature of inheritance. The red seals that once held the title of thousands of generations now hold memory. The faces emerge from binary like ghosts learning a new language for haunting.

The Matrix Prays in Pearls and Pixels, 2025

What do machines dream of when they dream of grace? The green-lit realm suggests that digital existence might not be an escape from the sacred– but another site for its emergence. The circuit board wears devotional objects like jewels—rosary beads, vintage photographs, a pixelated lily that refuses photorealism. Two figures rendered in code– might be amidst prayer or processing…The distinction collapses.

If consciousness can be uploaded, can reverence? If community forms in virtual spaces, does the divine meet us there too?

The green glow asks: is transcendence a place we go or a frequency we attune to?

Pater Noster (Corrupted File), 2025

The Lord's Prayer fragments across the frame like a corrupted transmission– "Pater Noster" glitching into visibility and absence simultaneously. Medieval manuscript illumination collides with digital decay– All sacred texts have always been vulnerable to the technologies that preserve them. The hands reach across centuries and mediums, seeking something that language, whether Latin or binary, can only approximate. What remains when the signal degrades? Not nothing…but perhaps the acknowledgment that holiness has always lived in the space between perfect transmission and total loss. We have never possessed the divine clearly; we have only ever reached for it through static.

Crucifixion.exe: Transubstantiation of Silicon+Flesh, 2025

At the intersection of suffering and innovation: a crucified Christ rendered in wire and circuitry, surrounded by the tools of creation– DNA helices, embryonic forms, geometric abstractions of matter itself.

If we are made in God's image, what happens when we remake ourselves?

The bioengineered future hovers here like a question mark made of flesh and data. Neural networks trace the same pattern as neural tissue. The cross becomes a circuit board becomes a medical diagram becomes the fundamental architecture of both organic and artificial life. Perhaps the most heretical thought: that to alter the code of life is not to play God but to continue the work of incarnation– spirit forever seeking new forms of embodiment.

The Body Electric Bleeds in Binary, 2025

An anatomical human body overlaid with system architecture—the sacred heart exposed next to literal code strings.

Where does the soul reside in a mapped and quantified body?

The flowers bloom in impossible colors, too saturated to be natural, too organic to be fully synthetic. Medical illustration meets religious iconography meets computational logic, and the boundaries between them dissolve. We thought dissection would demystify divinity, that scientific understanding would replace spiritual wonder. Instead, the deeper we look into the machinery of existence– whether biological or digital– the more intricate the mystery becomes.

The body was always electric. The machine was always, in some sense, alive.
